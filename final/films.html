🌽REFERENCE🌽

rottentomatoes

Rotten Tomatoes is a popular review aggregation website for films and television shows. It calculates a "Tomatometer" score based on the percentage of professional critic reviews that are positive for a given movie or TV show. The site also includes a "Popcornmeter" score, which aggregates audience ratings. 🔗

Here's a more detailed look:

Tomatometer : This score, displayed as a percentage, reflects the proportion of positive reviews from a network of professional critics. Rotten Tomatoes considers a score of 60% or higher "fresh" and anything below "rotten". 🔗

TPopcornmeter : This score represents the percentage of audience ratings that are positive, offering a gauge of how the general public is enjoying a film or show. 🔗

Certified Fresh : A coveted label awarded to movies with a high Tomatometer score (75% or higher) and a minimum number of reviews, including a certain number from "Top Critics". 🔗

Review Aggregation : Rotten Tomatoes gathers reviews from a wide range of sources, including online publications, newspapers, magazines, and individual critics, and then aggregates them into a single score. 🔗

Inspiration : The name "Rotten Tomatoes" is inspired by the practice of audiences throwing rotten tomatoes at bad stage performances. 🔗
